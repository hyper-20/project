The History of Turkey

The beginning of Turkey’s history is linked to the end of the Ottoman Empire. After the First World War, the Ottoman Empire lost the war with Germany, which was the central power, in 1918, and the territories were occupied by European countries. At that time, Mustafa Kemal (Mustafa Kemal) led the liberation movements. The Ottoman Empire was founded by Osman I in 129, and Asia, Eastern Europe Subjugated various regions in Africa. After the fall of Constantinople in 1453, he became one of the most powerful rulers in Europe.The most powerful period of the Ottoman period was after the 16th century, and from the 18th century onwards, rival countries such as European countries were approaching their weaknesses. In the early 19th century, the internal affairs of neighboring countries became more complicated, and after the Balkan Wars (1912–1913), many areas controlled by the Ottoman Empire changed. Especially during World War I (1914–1918), the Ottoman Empire and Germany After the war, the Allied Powers (United States, Britain, France) won the Treaty of Sèvres in 1920, ending the entire Ottoman Empire rule. At that time, Mustafa Kemal (Mustafa Kemal) led the liberation movements.

In 1919, he settled in Ankara and began to manage the Turkish War of Independence. In 1923, the war was won, the Ottoman Empire collapsed and the Republic of Turkey was established.Mustafa Kemal Ataturk became the first president of Turkey in 1923. During his administration, the national and political balance of Turkey was quite improved. His main reforms were: changing the country’s chronology: by adopting the Julian calendar, he separated religion and government and promoted secularism. Reforming the education system: he made education independent and greatly improved the citizens’ literary knowledge. Atatürk’s name means “Father of Turkey” and he was admired by the Turkish people at every national occasion.After Atatürk’s time, Turkey gradually implemented democratic institutions. Atatürk continued to follow the principles, but the strength of the army and the conflicts between the people became. But the military seized power from time to time and undermined the democratic system. In 1980, there were military coups and Turkey’s political system came under official control.

In the 1990s and 2000s, Turkey implemented many projects of political education and national development.Recep Tayyip Erdogan is the president of Turkey until 2024. He started as deputy prime minister in 2003 and stabilized as president in 2014. Apdogan’s administration period: Political reform: Apdogan built a subordinate government system without the confusion of power. In 2018, he changed the federal presidential system to a subordinate power system. Economic development: Under his government, the economy improved, especially engineering, He was particularly successful in real estate and international trade. International Relations: Apdogan was particularly active in Turkey’s international relations. He carried out more successful policies in government relations with his neighbors. Erdogan’s rule caused events between his authoritarian rule and free democracy, and Turkey became more and more transformed every time.
